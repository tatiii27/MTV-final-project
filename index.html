Guided Scrollytelling

Scroll through this guided section to see how each region's story shifts across decades.

Africa

In Sub-Saharan Africa, girls started far behind boys in secondary and tertiary school, with big gaps that narrowed but did not disappear by 2010–2020.

1960-1970

Very low access to secondary and university for both genders, with boys slightly ahead.

2010–2020

School access improved, but boys still keep an edge in both secondary and tertiary enrollment.

Asia

In South Asia, boys were far ahead in the 1960s. By 2010–2020, secondary gaps almost vanished, but women are only slightly behind in university.

1960-1970

Boys dominated secondary and especially tertiary enrollment.

2010–2020

Secondary is almost equal; university is close but still slightly favors men.

Middle East & North Africa

From low enrollment for everyone, the region moved toward high schooling with women now slightly ahead in university.

1960-1970

Secondary and tertiary education were limited, especially for girls.

2010–2020

Near-parity in secondary, but girls have slightly overtaken boys in tertiary.

Europe & Central Asia

Europe started with relatively high schooling and today reaches parity at secondary but sees girls dominate university enrollment.

1960-1970

Both genders had high secondary access; tertiary was roughly equal.

2010–2020

Secondary is at parity; girls are now the clear majority in university.

Latin America & Caribbean

Starting from relatively equal schooling, the region now sees girls ahead in both secondary and tertiary education.

1960-1970

Secondary was similar for both; boys had a small edge in tertiary.

2010–2020

Girls overtook boys in secondary and lead significantly in tertiary.

North America

From a gap favoring men in university during the 1960s, North America now sees women dominate tertiary enrollment.

1960-1970

Secondary data was limited; men heavily dominated tertiary enrollment.

2010–2020

Secondary is at parity; women now heavily outnumber men in university.

Oceania

From equal secondary but male-dominated tertiary in the 1960s, Oceania now has the strongest female advantage in university worldwide.

1960-1970

High secondary schooling for both, but men much more likely to attend university.

2010–2020

Secondary is very high; women become the clear majority in university.
